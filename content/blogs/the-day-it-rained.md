It started as a drizzle, a gentle tapping on the roof. By noon, the sky had turned a bruising shade of purple, and the streets were rivers.

### Washed Away

There's something cleansing about a downpour. It forces you indoors, forces you to pause. The noise of the city is drowned out by the relentless drumming of water against concrete.

I sat by the window, watching people scurry for cover. Umbrellas blossomed like colorful mushrooms. It was chaotic, yet strangely peaceful.

The rain didn't just wash away the dust; it washed away the urgency. For a few hours, the world stopped moving, and we were all just waiting for the sky to run out of tears.
